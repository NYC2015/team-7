Learn About HIV

Who can get HIV?
Anyone can contract HIV, regardless of income, gender, or sexual orientation.


When do symptoms of HIV start to show?
Symptoms can appear after as short as a few weeks or as long as 10 years.


How can I tell if I have HIV?
The only way to know if you have contracted HIV is to get an HIV test in the clinic.


How Long Can I Expect To Live With HIV?
If you take your medications regularly and live a healthy lifestyle, then your life expectancy is almost normal.


What should I do after testing positive for HIV?
If possible, you should consult with a doctor immediately in order to determine the progression of the virus and whether you should begin taking HIV medications. You may wish to join an HIV/AIDS support group and get more information from your local HIV organization.


How does HIV testing work?
Common HIV tests look for signs of the virus in your blood or check your oral fluids for low levels of antibodies.


When should I get tested for HIV?
If you think you were exposed to HIV, you should wait for two months before being tested, since you might test "negative" for HIV for up to two months after contracting the virus.


What does a positive test result mean?
A positive test result means that you are infected with the HIV virus; testing positive does NOT necessarily mean that you have AIDS. Many people who test positive stay healthy for several years, even if they don't start taking medication right away.


How accurate are the tests?
The HIV test is 99.5% accurate.


Is it okay to miss a dose of medication?
It is strongly recommended that you take your HIV medication regularly. If you have been on HIV medication for a long time, however, and the virus is completely suppressed in your body, then it typically takes 7-10 days for the virus to rebound. Is is especially important to take your medication regularly at the start of your treatment, before the virus has been suppressed.


Do I need to take my medication at the same time each day?
It is recommended that you take your HIV medication around the same time each day, but it is much more important that you take the medication every day


Does HIV/medication cause infertility?
There may be a slight incease in infertility rates among HIV-positive women on medications, but the vast majority of women of childbearing age can still become pregnant.
